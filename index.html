About Me

I'm an Electronics & Communication Engineering final-year student at RGUKT Srikakulam with a deep focus on robotics and reinforcement learning. My work lives at the intersection of embedded systems and intelligent control.

Currently a Research Intern at IIT Mandi's Center for AI & Robotics Lab, where I develop RL environments for 6-DOF manipulators in Isaac Lab, formulate energy-aware reward functions, and engineer sim-to-real pipelines.

I'm passionate about closing the gap between simulation and physical hardware — building policies that are not just smart in sim, but reliable on real robots. From Arduino motor control to MAPPO-based dual-arm cooperation, I enjoy working across the full stack.

Experience

Research Intern
Center for AI & Robotics Lab — IIT Mandi
May 2025 – Present
Developed RL environments for 6-DOF manipulators in Isaac Lab, addressing non-spherical wrist kinematics where standard analytical IK decoupling is computationally complex.
Implemented joint velocity control using PPO, formulating energy-aware reward functions to minimize oscillations and ensure smooth control transitions for physical hardware.
Engineered a Sim-to-Real pipeline by analyzing the impact of simulation frequencies and contact dynamics on policy convergence and stability.
Formulated Dual-Arm Cooperative IK using MAPPO, enforcing relative pose and antipodal constraints for synchronized end-effector alignment in collaborative manipulation tasks.
Integrated safety-critical constraints including joint limit avoidance and self-collision barriers within the continuous action space to guarantee operational safety.
